The History and Evolution of Birthday Emojis

While the concept of expressing emotions with symbols dates back to the 19th century, the modern emoji as we know it has a more recent history. Emojis like the birthday cake and party popper are part of this journey.

  • **Origins of Emojis**: The first emoticons, made from punctuation marks, were published in a humor magazine in 1881. The modern emoji set was created by Japanese designer Shigetaka Kurita in 1999 for a mobile platform.
  • **Worldwide Adoption**: Emojis gained global popularity when Apple added an official emoji keyboard to iOS in 2011, with Android following suit a few years later.
  • **Unicode Standard**: The Unicode Consortium, a non-profit that maintains text standards, now adds new emojis each year, ensuring they can be used across different devices and platforms.
